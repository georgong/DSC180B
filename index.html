The Challenge

Short-term Feasibility vs. Systemic Health

Why traditional scheduling pipelines miss the bigger picture.

⚠️

The Problem

[cite_start]

Current pipelines prioritize short-term rule satisfaction. This leads to "invisible" long-term risks like overdue accumulation, regional congestion, and uneven workload distribution that only emerge over time[cite: 15].

🌐

The Solution

A "World Model" that treats scheduling as an interconnected system. [cite_start]We evaluate candidate schedules not just on validity, but on probabilistic risk, duration deviation, and resilience[cite: 18].

📊

Data Representation

[cite_start]

Constructed a heterogeneous graph from the CLICK dataset: 5.4M task nodes and 22k engineer nodes, linked by operational logic to capture hidden dependencies[cite: 19, 70].

Our Approach

Graph-based World Model

Leveraging GNNs to capture structural and temporal dependencies.

Baseline: MLP

Non-graph baseline treating tasks independently. [cite_start]Used to benchmark the value of structural information[cite: 92].

RGCN

[cite_start]

Relational Graph Convolutional Networks. Distinguished semantically different interactions (e.g., assignment vs. geographic)[cite: 95].

GraphSAGE

[cite_start]

Scalable Inductive Learning. Aggregates neighborhood features to capture local structural context efficiently[cite: 101].

HGT

[cite_start]

Heterogeneous Graph Transformer. Uses attention mechanisms to weigh messages based on node and edge types[cite: 107].
